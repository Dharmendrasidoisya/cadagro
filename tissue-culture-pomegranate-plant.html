Tissue Culture Pomegranate Plant

General Information

Pomegranate (Punica sp.) is native to the Middle East and South Asia. The pomegranate is one of the first five cultivated foods in the world. The pomegranate is very high in potassium, vitamin C and anti-oxidants. Pomegranate is a high value crop. Apart from its demand for fresh fruits and juice, the processed products like wine and candy are also gaining importance in world trade. It is an ideal crop for the sustainability of small holdings, as pomegranate is well suited to the topography and agro-climate of arid and semi-arid regions

Cultivation Practice & Yield

  • Soil : Soils should be fertile and well drained. Sandy loam to black soil, rich in organic matter > 20% are preferred.
  • Climate : Red Pearl prefers cool winter and dry hot summer.
  • Variety : Bhagwa or Sinduri.
  • Season of planting : Feb – March (summer), June – July (monsoon) and September-October (Early winter).
  • Spacing : Spacing of 5 M x 4 M is desirable for proper growth of crop plants.
  • Days to flowering : 1 year to 1½ years.
  • Harvesting : Red Pearl starts flowering after 1½ to 2 years after 4-5 months of flowering, fruits are mature for harvesting.
  • Yield* : For initial 3 years 20 to 50 fruits per plant, which increase up to 150-200 fruits after 5 years. (30-40 kgs / tree)

* varies with field conditions
